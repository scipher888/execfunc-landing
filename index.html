Future Vision

Support-person escalation

For tasks you mark as critical, the escalation chain can optionally reach out to a designated support person — a partner, friend, or family member — if you're not responding. Fully user-controlled, blockable at any time, with pre-notification before any outreach. This feature is in development and will be available for consenting adults only.

What You Get

A framework, not another app

ExecFunc is a set of skills and design principles for OpenClaw. You bring your own AI, your own messaging app, your own integrations.

🧠

AI Companion With Memory

Not a faceless notification system. A persistent AI that remembers your patterns, preferences, and priorities across sessions. One personality, one relationship.

📈

Escalating Reminders

Gentle text → firmer nudge → direct message with options. Each level is user-configured and can be disabled. Because sometimes you need more than one notification.

📋

Morning Briefings

Wake up to a personalized summary: your calendar, pending tasks, conflicts detected, and a realistic assessment of your day. No fluff, just what matters.

⚡

Friction Reduction

Your AI handles the overhead — scheduling conflicts, administrative pile-ups, the insurance email you've been avoiding for a week. Less friction between intention and action.

🔒

Anti-Capture by Design

Proactive message volume caps, no engagement optimization, no dark patterns. The goal is better functioning — not dependency, not habit formation, not "engagement."

🔧

Self-Hosted & Private

Your data stays on your hardware. No cloud accounts, no telemetry, no third-party data sharing. MIT licensed. Fork it, modify it, make it yours.

Origin Story

Built by a family, for families

This started as a startup business plan. After rigorous adversarial review and honest self-assessment, the founder — a practicing health care provider with a family — realized the right move was to build it for his own family first, prove it works, and share it with anyone who wants it.

We're testing every feature in a real household before publishing anything. The design documents are available now. Skills and code are rolling out as they're validated.

The startup option isn't closed. But the evidence needs to come first. We believe that's how software for people who are struggling should be built — carefully, honestly, and with the people it's for.
